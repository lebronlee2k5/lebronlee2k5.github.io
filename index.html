From Asthma Attacks to Attack on Deadlines:
My Life’s Unexpected Journey

MY EARLY CHILDHOOD AND CHALLENGES

     I am Lebron Lee C. Setosta and I live in Tondo, Manila.1 I was born on September 23, 2005 at Jose R. Reyes Memorial Medical Center in Tondo, Manila but not long after I was born we moved to Binmaley, Pangasinan.2 We moved because my parents separated and I moved with my mom along with my older sister.3 I was raised in Binmaley, Pangasinan until I was 6 years old.4 I spent half of my childhood in Pangasinan with my mom and my grandparents on my mother's side.5 In those 6 years, I am so weak that most of the time I am sick.6 I cannot play outside for long with other children because I have asthma and I cannot get too tired from playing.7 I remember one day when I was playing more than usual.8 Later that day, I had a serious asthma attack, and I know my mom and grandparents were up all night, worried sick and making sure I was okay.9 Living in the province without proper work and income was hard.10 Our only income was from selling necessary daily things at the market, with a family where there are many members, that income was so little.11 Although, living there was fun for a child with no knowledge about expenses but for the adults it was hard.12 At that time my father was not supporting us financially at all, making it more difficult for my mom.13 I also started my education that time and my sister is at High School.14 I finished my kindergarten, preparatory and grade one in Pangasinan.15 Since life was challenging in the province, my mom sought opportunities elsewhere to support my sister and me.16 She decided to work overseas and applied for a job in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia.17 2012 was the year my mom went to Saudi Arabia. I was little then, and since I didn't know where she was going, I kind of felt like she'd just left and didn't love us anymore.18 Around that time, we moved back to Manila, and I also met my father for the first time.19 Since my mom was going to work abroad, they agreed that we would live with him.20

MY TEENAGE YEARS AND GROWTH

     Years later, I learned that my mom didn't say goodbye because she knew I wouldn't understand and would just cry and beg her to stay.1 I was an early teen when I saw my mom again.2 It was after I graduated from my Elementary education.3 My teenage life is not as boring as my childhood.4 I don’t get sick that much anymore, I made a lot of new friends, met a lot of amazing people, fell in love, experienced heartbreak and many new experiences that shaped the current me5 I remember being in seventh grade and experiencing that whole "first love" thing.6 It began with just admiring her from afar, but soon I was completely smitten.7 Seeing her in the hallways would make my heart race.8 I tried to confess and pursue her but she rejected me, it was my first rejection and also my first heartbreak.9 Although she rejected me, my feelings for her stayed, years have passed and I still admired her from afar.10 In my eighth grade days, my mind became more open, I started doing things for fun.11 Doing things such as joining school section competitions and joining intramural sports competitions.12 Eighth grade was also a big year for me – I made the top 10 for the first time in high school.13 I'd missed too much attendance in seventh grade to qualify, but I was determined to do better.14 I was proud of myself that time.15 In my ninth grade I am more active, because I want to do more things that I could be proud of, but before my ninth grade ends.16 Pandemic happened, during the pandemic a lot of things have changed.17 It feels like my teenage years were wasted during that time.18 I graduated my Junior High School online and to me it feels like not an achievement.19 I enrolled as an ICT strand student, I started and ended my eleventh grade via online class, because of the pandemic it feels like time skipped and made me a grade 12.20 In my twelfth grade, life is starting to go back to normal and I graduated my Senior High with Honors and Best in Research Award.21

MY COLLEGE LIFE AND ASPIRATIONS

     Now, I am in College.1 I am studying at University of the East Manila, under the program of Bachelor of Science in Information Technology.2 I am a second year student.3 In my freshman year, I was anxious and scared in College life.4 I am still not over with my SHS years.5 I am still processing what happened during and after the pandemic.6 To me it feels too fast that I cannot keep up with what is happening.7 Though I am scared, I face the challenge and accept that I am at a college.8 Thanks to the friends I made in my freshman year, the heaviness of my chosen program feels a little lighter.9 The courses and subjects in my program are hard.10 Especially when you cannot understand the lessons.11 I need to study twice as hard to keep up with my blockmates.12 Now I am in sophomore year, I could say that I am fully adapted to college life.13 Though the subjects are much harder than my freshman year.14 I think I could survive it and move into the next level.15 The experiences that I gained during my freshman year helped me a lot.16 The events that happen from time to time in school also helps me to enjoy my college life.17 I'm really looking forward to graduation and imagining what I'll become.18 Achieving my dreams would be the greatest accomplishment of my life.19 My own motivation is what keeps me going.20
